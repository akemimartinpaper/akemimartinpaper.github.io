About

The Maker

Akemi Martin is a professional papermaker, artist, and adjunct professor of art. Her practice lives at the intersection of ancient craft and contemporary thought — pulling fiber from water, pressing it into form, letting it dry into something that holds meaning before a single mark is made.

She teaches, makes, and thinks out loud. Her studio is part laboratory, part garden, part chaos.

Introduction to Papermaking

Pull your first sheet from the vat. Learn the basics of Western and Japanese techniques, fiber prep, and deckle use. No experience needed — just curiosity.

The Process

Process

Why I pull paper by hand when machines exist

There is something that happens between the water and the fiber and your hands that no machine can replicate. It's not nostalgia. It's physics. It's attention. It's the small decisions that make something alive.

Materials

Foraging fiber: what grows near you

Nettles, iris leaves, banana peels, old jeans. Everything is potential pulp if you're willing to cook it down.
